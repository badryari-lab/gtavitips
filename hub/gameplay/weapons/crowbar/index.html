Crow Bar

It opens doors. It also closes conversations. The crowbar does not distinguish between its two primary functions and neither does anyone using it correctly.

Lethality
Medium-High
Steel construction delivers more impact force per swing than wooden alternatives. Durable through extended use.
Effective Range
Close Range
Effective reach slightly longer than a bat. The hooked end extends usable striking geometry.
Construction
Steel
Single piece forged steel. No structural failure under field conditions. The same tool pries a lock and handles a confrontation.
Technical Intelligence

Dossier

The crowbar sits in the tool category legally and the weapon category practically. In Leonida, where the line between a job that requires equipment and a job that requires a weapon is blurry at the best of times, that dual function is the point.

It pries secured doors, breaks padlocks, and handles opposition that does not have the good sense to leave. No reload. No ammunition. No maintenance beyond keeping it in the vehicle.

VCPD catalogues it as a burglary tool when recovered at a scene. That classification understates its field applications considerably.

Frequently Asked

Frequently Asked

What's the Crowbar's real use in a Leonida operation?
Access first. Doors, locks, crates. And when the situation requires more than that, it handles quiet corrections at close range without leaving anything traceable.
Where can you get the Crowbar in Leonida?
Ammu-Nation is the confirmed weapon retailer in Leonida — Phil Cassidy's name is on the billboard. Specific stock, pricing, and unlock conditions aren't confirmed yet.
Does it show up in specific operations or locations?
Contextually it fits Keys and rural Leonida operations — anywhere forced entry is more useful than a firearm. The On Record section covers the confirmed appearances.
