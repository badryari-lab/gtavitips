GTA 6 Trailer 2: The Full Intelligence Breakdown

Three minutes. The full map. This is everything Trailer 2 confirmed about Leonida, organized by category and linked to the full database.

Rockstar May 2025
The Document

What Trailer 2 Actually Is

Trailer 2 dropped May 6, 2025. It ran just over three minutes. Where Trailer 1 introduced the world through the protagonists, Trailer 2 spread outward. More locations, more vehicles, more factions, more activities. It is less a story trailer and more an intelligence dump. The world is larger than Trailer 1 showed, and Trailer 2 spent its runtime demonstrating that.

This breakdown organizes what Trailer 2 confirmed by category and links every entity to its full database entry. Trailer 1 established the foundation. Trailer 2 built on it.

Source
All intelligence below is sourced from Trailer 2 only. the official Rockstar release of May 6, 2025.
Locations

The Map Gets Bigger

Trailer 2 confirms what Trailer 1 implied: Leonida is a state with serious geographic range. The Leonida Keys are the most significant new location confirmed in the trailer. The island chain, the shallow reef channels, the open water off the outer keys, and the specific environment that separates the Keys from the mainland are all visible. It is the part of the map that makes fishing, diving, kayaking, and seaplane operations possible in ways the mainland does not support.

Port Gellhorn is confirmed through port infrastructure sequences and the waterfront commercial environment that Raul Bautista operates within. The port is the cargo entry point for Leonida's import economy, legitimate and otherwise.

Inland, the Mount Kalaga territory is expanded. Elevated roads, the descent terrain for mountain drifting, and the wilderness infrastructure that separates the Vice County interior from the coastal zone. The Red Hill Forest and Lake Leonida are both confirmed in interior sequences.

Factions

The Criminal Ecosystem in Full

Trailer 2 is where most of the faction confirmations land. The 7X crew is more visible here than in Trailer 1. their social media operations, their Vice City street presence, and the specific car culture they operate through are all present. High Rollerz appear in the racing circuit sequences on the Vice City waterfront. The Outlaw Motorcycle Gang is visible on the Vice County highway network.

Law enforcement confirmations expand significantly. The VCSO in Vice County suburban and rural sequences. The Vice County SWAT in high-wanted-level sequences. Animal Control in a Vice County incident response. The public service infrastructure of Leonida is visible and populated.

Analysis
The animal control appearance in Trailer 2 is one of the more overlooked confirmations. It establishes that Leonida's wildlife incidents are managed by a dedicated agency. In a state with confirmed alligators, pythons, and a Florida panther population, Animal Control has functional relevance. It is not a background detail.
Vehicles

The Roster Expands

Trailer 2 confirms a broader vehicle roster than Trailer 1 across every category. Performance vehicles visible include the Grotti Furia at a Vice City valet stand and the Zentorno in a car meet context. The Karin Sultan RS is confirmed in the multi-level parking deck sequence that became one of the most shared frames from the trailer.

Off-road and rural vehicles are confirmed for the first time. The mudding sequence in rural Vice County directly confirms lifted truck culture and the mudding activity. The Maibatsu Sanchez dirt bike in Vice County terrain establishes off-road motorcycle access to the rural interior.

Watercraft expand the map: the Pegassi Speeder in Keys waters, the Shitzu Longfin center console fishing boat, and the Nagasaki Dinghy in coastal channels confirm that the Keys geography is fully navigable by water.

Activities

What Leonida Lets You Do

Trailer 2 is where the activities database largely originates. Fishing is confirmed at a Leonida waterway. Kayaking in the Keys backcountry. Diving in the Keys underwater environment. Mudding in rural Vice County. Racing through the High Rollerz circuit. Gambling in a Vice City casino interior.

The range of confirmed activities tells you something about the game's geographic spread. Fishing and kayaking require the Keys and the inland waterways. Mudding requires rural Vice County. Racing requires Vice City streets and the industrial waterfront. The activity set is not concentrated in one part of the map. It maps to the full geographic range of Leonida.

Analysis
The balance between criminal activities and leisure activities in Trailer 2 is notably even. Equal screen time goes to robbery and fishing, to racing and kayaking. This is a deliberate signal about what kind of game Leonida is. The criminal premise does not consume the world. The world existed before the crime and will exist around it.
What It Added

Trailer 1 Built the Foundation. Trailer 2 Built the World.

The shift from Trailer 1 to Trailer 2 is a shift in scope. Trailer 1 is intimate. Two characters, one city, a criminal premise and a relationship. Trailer 2 opens to the full map. The Keys, the port, the forest, the rural interior, the mountain terrain. The full faction roster across criminal organisations, law enforcement, and public services. The vehicle roster across performance, off-road, watercraft, and commercial categories.

What both trailers together confirm is a game built around geographic and social density. The world is large. The world is populated. The world has things to do that are not connected to the story. Leonida is not a backdrop for a story. It is the story's context and its substance simultaneously.
