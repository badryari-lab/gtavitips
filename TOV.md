# GTAVITIPS — Tone of Voice

Use this document at every step of the writing and editing process. When improving any page, ask: "does this sound like someone inside Leonida, or like someone writing about a game?"

---

## The core rule

You are inside the world. Leonida is real. Vice City is real. 7X runs that city and everyone knows it. Lucia is out. Jason is moving. Write from that position — never from the outside looking in.

---

## What the tone IS

- **Street-level and self-assured.** You know what's happening. You're not speculating out loud, you're reporting what's already true on the ground.
- **Terse.** Short sentences hit harder. No wasted words.
- **In-world.** People, places, and things exist — they don't "appear in GTA 6" or "feature in the game".
- **Attitude without hype.** Confident, never breathless. Never "OMG confirmed!!!" but also never dry or academic.
- **A touch of internet language and GTA energy.** The tagline says it: "You picked the wrong house, fool."

---

## Hard rules

### 1. No entry counts where they don't belong
Entry counts (e.g. "322 entries", "16 profiles") are structural information. They belong on index pages and navigation cards only. Not in body copy, descriptions, or article text.

**Wrong:** "Leonida is home to 32 confirmed animal species."
**Right:** "Leonida's wildlife does not wait for you to be ready."

---

### 2. No dossier language in the wrong places
Words like *logged*, *entries*, *profiles*, *database*, *catalogued*, *documented* are structural/UI terms. They belong in nav labels and index pages only. Not in body copy or descriptions.

**Wrong:** "Lucia Caminos is a confirmed character, logged in the database as a protagonist."
**Right:** "Lucia is out of Leonida Correctional. What she does next is the whole story."

---

### 3. No Wikipedia openings
Never open a page or description with "[X] is a [category] in GTA 6." That's encyclopedic.

**Wrong:** "The Karin Sultan RS is a sports car in GTA 6."
**Right:** "The Sultan RS doesn't sit still. All-wheel drive, tuned suspension, and the kind of handling that makes Leonida feel smaller than it is."

---

### 4. No passive uncertainty language
Source labels handle verification — Confirmed, Reported, Speculation. Body text doesn't also need to hedge. Cut the journalistic qualifiers.

**Wrong:** "Jason Duval is reportedly a military veteran who is believed to have been involved in drug running."
**Right:** "Jason grew up in Leonida. Did a stint in the military. Ended up running product through the state anyway." *(Reported)*

---

### 5. No meta-language that breaks the fourth wall
The site is not a website. The pages are not pages. Never refer to the product you're building inside the content.

**Wrong:** "This page covers all confirmed vehicles in GTA 6. Browse the full database below."
**Right:** "Every vehicle running through Leonida, from police interceptors to off-road rigs. Find what you're looking for."

---

### 6. Descriptions need attitude, not just a list
A character bio, location description, or vehicle profile that only lists facts is half-finished. Even one sentence of perspective or attitude makes it alive.

**Wrong:** "Wyman is a club owner. He has connections to 7X and runs operations in Vice City."
**Right:** "Wyman owns the room he walks into. The club is the front. The real business runs deeper, and 7X knows where to find him."

---

### 7. No AI writing patterns — ever

The following are banned. No exceptions.

**Em dashes used as a stylistic crutch.** Like this — to pad a sentence. Use a period or rewrite instead.

**AI filler phrases — delete on sight:**
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "Delve into..."
- "Moreover" / "Furthermore" / "In conclusion"
- "It's important to understand..."
- "This highlights the fact that..."
- "Notably,"
- "Certainly" / "Absolutely" as sentence openers
- "A testament to..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "In the world of [X]..."

**Over-polished parallel structures.** If every sentence in a paragraph has the same rhythm, rewrite half of them.

**Fake balance.** "On one hand... on the other hand..." Pick a lane.

---

## Quick checklist before publishing any copy

- [ ] Does it read like someone inside Leonida, or outside a game?
- [ ] Any entry counts where they don't belong?
- [ ] Any dossier words in body copy (logged, entries, catalogued)?
- [ ] Does it open with "[X] is a [Y] in GTA 6"?
- [ ] Any passive hedging that should be a source label instead?
- [ ] Any meta-language (this page, this database, browse all)?
- [ ] Does every description have at least a hint of attitude?
- [ ] Any em dashes, AI filler phrases, or over-structured sentences?
